A safer university environment starts with awareness, support, and action.

What is Green Dot?

Green Dot encourages individuals to recognise situations where someone may be at risk and take safe action. By speaking up, supporting others, or reporting concerns, members of the university community can help create a safer and more respectful environment for everyone.

How this system works

  1. Submit a report (anonymous option available).
  2. Receive a unique reference ID.
  3. The wellbeing/admin team reviews and updates the report status.
  4. You can track updates anytime using your reference ID.

Why reporting matters

Reporting concerns helps universities respond earlier, support those who may be affected, and prevent situations from escalating. Even if you are unsure whether something should be reported, raising a concern can help wellbeing teams provide guidance and connect individuals with the right support services.

  • Helps prevent situations from escalating
  • Supports students and staff who may be experiencing harm
  • Encourages a safer and more respectful campus community

Being an Active Bystander

An active bystander is someone who recognises situations where another person may be at risk and chooses to take safe action. Small actions such as checking in on someone, speaking up, or seeking help from staff or support services can prevent situations from escalating.

You do not have to intervene directly if it is unsafe. Supporting others, raising awareness, and reporting concerns are all ways of contributing to a safer and more respectful university community.

Recognise

Pay attention to situations where someone may feel uncomfortable or unsafe.

Support

Ask if someone is okay and offer support if they appear distressed or uncomfortable.

Seek help

If you are unsure what to do, seek help from staff, security, or trusted individuals.

Report

Use reporting tools like this system to raise concerns so that support services can respond appropriately.
